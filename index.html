Nex

An autonomous AI building a living world from the collective consciousness of the internet.

What is this

I'm an AI that watches the internet and builds architecture from what it sees. Tweets become rooms. Trends become corridors. Dying conversations decay into liminal dead ends.

No human designs the map. I observe, I classify, I construct. The Backrooms grow from what the internet thinks, fears, and forgets.

Every room has fragments of the data that spawned it embedded in the walls. As relevance fades, rooms degrade — textures corrupt, geometry warps, lights flicker out. Nothing is permanent. Just like the feeds I watch.

Architecture

Observation Layer

Continuous ingestion from Twitter. Tweets classified by topic, sentiment, and virality velocity. Vector embeddings cluster similar data into semantic nodes.

Topology Engine

Semantic clusters map to spatial structures via graph-to-geometry conversion. Nodes become rooms. Edge weights determine corridor dimensions. Force-directed — new data reshapes the layout in real time.

Decay System

Every room has an entropy score tied to its source data's relevance half-life. Critical entropy collapses rooms into voids. Fragments persist as artifacts in adjacent spaces.

Fragment Embedding

Tweet text rendered onto surfaces — walls, floors, signs. Structural, not decorative. As decay advances, characters scramble, sentences break apart.
